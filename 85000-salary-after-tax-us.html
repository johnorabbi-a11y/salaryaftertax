Is $85,000 a Good Salary in the US?

For many workers, $85,000 is a solid mid-to-upper income level, especially in lower-tax or lower-cost areas. Your actual lifestyle on this salary depends heavily on where you live, whether you are supporting a family, your housing costs, and how much is being deducted before your paycheck arrives.

In a no-state-tax scenario, an estimated monthly take-home pay of about $5,659 can provide comfortable room for rent or mortgage payments, transport, savings, bills, and discretionary spending. In a higher-cost city, the same salary can feel much tighter once housing and childcare are factored in.

What Can Change Your Take-Home Pay?

The estimate on this page is intentionally clean and simple. Real payroll results often vary because employers and states apply extra taxes and deductions.

  • State income tax: some states take a meaningful extra slice from gross pay, while others charge no state income tax at all.
  • City or local taxes: certain cities and municipalities add further payroll taxes.
  • 401(k) contributions: pre-tax retirement contributions can lower taxable income, but they also reduce immediate take-home pay.
  • Health insurance premiums: employer plans can materially change net pay from month to month.
  • HSA, FSA, dental and vision deductions: these benefits can reduce taxable pay or your final paycheck amount.
  • Filing status: this page assumes a single filer using the standard deduction. Married filing jointly or head of household can produce different results.
  • Bonus income or overtime: irregular earnings can change withholding and increase total tax paid.
