Is $90,000 a Good Salary in the US?

For many households, $90,000 is a strong salary that can support a comfortable standard of living, especially in areas with lower housing costs and no state income tax. In higher-cost regions, the same income can still feel tight once housing, childcare, transport, and insurance are included.

In this simplified example, estimated take-home pay comes to just under $5,951 per month. That can give you solid room for bills, savings, and discretionary spending, but the real outcome depends heavily on location and payroll deductions.

What Can Change Your Take-Home Pay?

The estimate on this page is intended as a clean federal baseline. Your actual paycheck can vary for several reasons.

  • State income tax: some states take a noticeable extra slice from your wages, while others do not tax income at all.
  • City or local tax: certain local areas apply further taxes on earnings.
  • 401(k) contributions: retirement deductions can lower taxable income but reduce cash in your bank account now.
  • Health insurance and workplace benefits: medical, dental, and vision deductions can reduce net pay meaningfully.
  • Filing status: this page assumes a single filer using the standard deduction. Different filing statuses can change results.
  • Bonuses and overtime: extra earnings may change withholding and total tax paid over the year.
