Why monthly take-home pay is lower than expected

At a salary of £337,000, your monthly gross pay is high, but the UK tax system applies substantial deductions. Additional-rate tax, higher-rate tax, and National Insurance all reduce the amount that reaches your bank account.

Your personal allowance is also lost at this income level, which means more of your earnings are fully taxable compared with lower salaries.

What can change your monthly net pay?

  • Pension contributions: these may reduce taxable salary and lower tax due.
  • Bonus timing: if part of your income is paid as bonus, some months may show heavier deductions.
  • Student loans: repayments can noticeably reduce monthly take-home pay.
  • Benefits in kind: private medical cover, cars, and similar perks can affect your tax code.
  • Salary sacrifice schemes: these can improve tax efficiency depending on setup.
