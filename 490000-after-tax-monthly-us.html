What affects take-home pay on $490,000 a year?

At this salary, relatively small planning changes can move take-home pay by a meaningful amount. State income tax is usually the biggest missing variable in a federal-only estimate, especially if you are comparing a no-tax state with a high-tax state. Filing jointly instead of filing single can also change the effective federal burden. Pre-tax retirement contributions, health coverage, HSA deposits, and deferred compensation plans can reduce taxable income and increase monthly take-home efficiency over time. Bonus-heavy compensation can create a very different withholding pattern from a straight salary too, so your actual paychecks may not match a simple evenly spread annual model.
