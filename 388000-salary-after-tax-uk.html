How tax works at this salary

At £388,000 per year, most of your income falls into the higher and additional rate tax bands. Your personal allowance is also fully removed at this level of income, which means more of your earnings are taxed.

National Insurance still applies, although at a lower marginal rate on earnings above the main threshold. That means your marginal tax rate remains high even though the National Insurance percentage falls.

What can change your take-home pay?

  • Pension contributions through salary sacrifice can reduce taxable income and improve efficiency.
  • Student loan repayments, bonus payments, company benefits, or private healthcare can change your net pay.
  • Your tax code may differ from the standard code used in simple estimates.
  • Scottish tax rates are different, so this page is most useful for standard UK taxpayer comparisons outside Scotland.
