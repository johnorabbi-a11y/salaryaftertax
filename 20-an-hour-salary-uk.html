£20 an Hour Salary (UK)

If you earn £20 per hour, your yearly pay depends on how many hours you work and how many weeks you’re paid for. Below are quick conversions using common UK assumptions — then you can run the exact numbers in our calculator.

Quick conversion table (common assumptions)

Assumption A: 40 hours/week, 52 weeks/year

  • Hourly: £20.00
  • Weekly: £800
  • Monthly: £3,466.67
  • Annual: £41,600

Monthly is annual ÷ 12. (Real pay schedules vary.)

Assumption B: 37.5 hours/week, 52 weeks/year

  • Weekly: £750
  • Monthly: £3,250.00
  • Annual: £39,000

Assumption C: 35 hours/week, 52 weeks/year

  • Weekly: £700
  • Monthly: £3,033.33
  • Annual: £36,400

What about take-home pay?

Your take-home pay depends on your tax code, pension contributions, student loan, and whether you’re paid hourly via PAYE. For a proper estimate, use the calculator and select UK.

How the maths works (simple)

  • Weekly pay = hourly rate × hours per week
  • Annual pay = weekly pay × paid weeks per year
  • Monthly pay = annual pay ÷ 12
