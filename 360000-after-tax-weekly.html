How is weekly net pay worked out?

The weekly figure is simply the estimated annual take-home pay divided by 52. Real payslips can vary slightly depending on payroll timing, bonuses, tax code changes and any workplace deductions that sit outside standard PAYE tax and employee National Insurance.

For a very high salary like £360,000, a large part of earnings sits in the additional rate tax band, so the weekly deduction figure is substantial compared with lower salary levels.

What can change your weekly pay?

  • Pension salary sacrifice
  • Student loan repayments
  • Bonus-heavy compensation structures
  • Company car or private healthcare benefits
  • Scottish tax residency
