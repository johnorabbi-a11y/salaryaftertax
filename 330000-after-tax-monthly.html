Why the monthly net pay is much lower than gross pay

On a salary of £330,000, a large share of earnings is taxed at the higher and additional rates. Once your income goes beyond the Personal Allowance taper zone, that allowance is fully removed, increasing the total tax paid.

That means your monthly payslip can look very different from your headline salary figure, especially at higher income levels.

What might affect your monthly result?

Your actual monthly take-home pay may be higher or lower depending on pension contributions, salary sacrifice, bonuses, student loan repayments, private medical cover, share schemes or other payroll adjustments.

Use this as a practical guide for estimation rather than an exact payslip calculation.
