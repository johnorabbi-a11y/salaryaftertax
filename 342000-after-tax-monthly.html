Why monthly net pay is lower

On a salary of £342,000, a large share of earnings is taxed at the top UK rates. Your personal allowance is also fully removed, which increases taxable income.

That means monthly take-home pay is still very strong, but well below the gross monthly figure.

What can change your monthly pay?

  • Pension contributions may lower taxable pay.
  • Bonuses can change deductions in some months.
  • Student loans can reduce monthly take-home pay.
  • Benefits in kind may affect your tax code.
  • Salary sacrifice can improve tax efficiency.
