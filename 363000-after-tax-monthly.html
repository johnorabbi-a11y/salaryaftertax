Why is monthly take-home pay lower than gross pay?

Once earnings move well above the higher and additional rate thresholds, each extra part of income is taxed at a higher marginal rate. On a £363,000 salary, the personal allowance is also fully lost, which pushes more income into taxable bands.

That is why the gap between gross monthly pay and net monthly pay is much wider than it is at lower salaries.

What can change your monthly net pay?

  • Salary sacrifice pension contributions
  • Student loan deductions each payslip
  • Private medical cover or other benefits in kind
  • Bonus timing and irregular payroll treatment
  • Scottish taxpayer status
