Why monthly net pay is lower

On a salary of £338,000, a large portion of your earnings is taxed at the upper UK tax bands. Your personal allowance is also fully removed, increasing the amount of taxable income.

That means your monthly take-home pay is still very strong, but well below the gross monthly salary figure.

What can change your monthly pay?

  • Pension contributions may lower taxable pay.
  • Bonuses can create different deductions in some months.
  • Student loans can reduce monthly take-home pay.
  • Benefits in kind may affect your tax code.
  • Salary sacrifice can improve tax efficiency in some cases.
